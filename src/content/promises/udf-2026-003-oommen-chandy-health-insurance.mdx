## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to a health insurance scheme named after the late Oommen Chandy that covers every household in Kerala for medical expenses of up to ₹25 lakh per year.

The scheme as named in the manifesto is structurally similar in branding to a 2023 announcement under the previous LDF government, but the manifesto sets a higher household coverage ceiling. We do not interpret the relationship between the two here; we record only what was promised.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the scheme is universal at the point of access, or operates as a reimbursement against private insurance for households above a certain income.
- The list of empanelled hospitals.
- The premium model: fully tax-funded, partially contributory, or a mix.
- Coverage scope: in-patient only, day-care, out-patient, prescription medicines, diagnostics.
- Interaction with existing schemes such as Karunya Arogya Suraksha Padhathi (KASP) and Ayushman Bharat.

We will record these as the government publishes the scheme design.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when the government issues an order constituting the scheme or appoints a committee to design it. It reaches `fulfilled` when the scheme is operational, an order specifies eligibility and coverage at the level the manifesto committed to, and verified evidence from a Tier 1 or Tier 2 source confirms that households are receiving the benefit.

A pilot in selected districts, or a rebranding of an existing scheme without raising the coverage ceiling, will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.

## Why this matters in plain terms

Out-of-pocket health expenditure is the single largest cause of household financial distress in India. A universal ₹25 lakh household coverage ceiling would be among the highest in the country, and the operational details — who is covered, where, for what — will determine whether the headline holds up at a hospital counter. The record matters.
