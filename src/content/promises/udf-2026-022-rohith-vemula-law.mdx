## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to enacting a law named after Rohith Vemula to address caste discrimination, framed in coverage as a campus-safety and student-welfare statute. Rohith Vemula was a research scholar at the University of Hyderabad whose 2016 death became the reference case for institutional caste discrimination on Indian campuses.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the Act will be a stand-alone statute or an amendment to existing higher-education legislation in Kerala.
- The scope: higher-education institutions only, or all educational institutions including schools.
- Penalties, complaint mechanisms, and the institutional structure for investigation.
- The relationship to existing Scheduled Caste / Scheduled Tribe atrocities legislation and to University Grants Commission anti-discrimination regulations.

We will record these as they appear in the Bill or in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a Bill is introduced in the Kerala Legislative Assembly. It reaches `fulfilled` when the Bill is passed, receives the Governor's assent, and is notified in the gazette.

A government order, executive notification, or a renaming of an existing cell will not count as fulfilment; the manifesto commits to a law.

## Why this matters in plain terms

Caste discrimination on Indian campuses is documented across decades and across institutions. A named Act is a measurable, durable commitment that outlasts any one administration's circulars. The record matters.
