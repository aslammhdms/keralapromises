# Peer trackers — competitive map

> Internal research document. Not published on the public site. Refresh as the landscape changes.

**Compiled:** 2026-05-21
**Method:** Direct fetches and web searches; sites that did not respond or were login-gated are noted as such.

This document records what other public-promise / manifesto trackers exist in India, with a focus on those that overlap with the Kerala Promises scope. It is research for the maintainers — not commentary, and not a public-facing page.

## What this means for the project

### Direct competitor

`politicalperformance.com` is the only site doing roughly what we do, in the same state, on the same manifesto. It is broader (LDF + UDF + NDA · 2021 + 2026) and further along (46 UDF 2026 pledges already loaded versus our 17). It is shallower on methodology (no published source hierarchy, no API, no licence, no Git history, single operator).

The defensible position for Kerala Promises is not speed or breadth — it is discipline. The four-state ontology in BRIEF §5.2, the Tier 1–4 source hierarchy, the gazette-or-on-ground requirement for `fulfilled`, the archive.org mirroring, and the Git-public audit trail are the things peer trackers do not have.

### Tier-1 opportunity

`manifesto.kerala.gov.in` is the government's own tracker. It currently records the outgoing LDF 2021 manifesto. The new UDF government will almost certainly inherit or rebuild this portal, and when it does, it becomes our highest-grade evidence source for `fulfilled` flips. Worth checking weekly. The `/refresh-tracker` slash command should include it as a first-pass Tier-1 check.

### Methodology sibling

`sarkarivaade.com` does not cover Kerala but its discipline is the closest to ours of anyone in this space. It is also a one-person project. Natural collaboration potential: cross-link, share contributors, possibly converge on a schema for state-level promise data.

### Unknown

`vakdhanam.in` is a single-page site with a tagline so close to our positioning that it merits ongoing observation. As of the compile date, the domain has no published ownership, no archived snapshots accessible to us, and no social-media or press trail. Three readings: pre-launch project, abandoned project, or deliberately anonymous project. We do not have evidence to choose between them.

## What we deliberately did not look at

- Promise trackers for other states with no Kerala scope (Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra). They are out of overlap.
- International civic-tech templates such as `promisetracker.dev.codeforafrica.org` and the Thai `wevisdemo/promise-tracker`. Useful as design references but not competitors.
- Generic news-aggregation or fact-check sites. Different category.

## Refresh cadence

Re-run this map every six months, or sooner if a peer tracker publishes a significant new feature, or if `manifesto.kerala.gov.in` re-anchors on UDF 2026.
