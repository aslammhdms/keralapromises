## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to two paired interventions: a Sidharthan Anti-Ragging & Student Welfare Act, and a Sidharthan Student Distress App. The promise is named after Sidharthan J. S., a veterinary college student whose death at a Wayanad campus hostel in February 2024 became a defining test case for ragging accountability in Kerala.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the Act will be a stand-alone statute or an amendment to the existing 1998 Kerala Prohibition of Ragging Act.
- Penalties, reporting timelines, and the institutional structure for investigation.
- The app's scope: whether it serves only ragging complaints or wider student-welfare grievances; which department hosts it; and how anonymity and follow-up are handled.

We will record these as they appear in the bill or in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise is in two parts, and we will track both. The Act reaches `in_progress` when a bill is introduced in the Assembly and `fulfilled` when it is passed and notified in the gazette. The app reaches `in_progress` when a development tender or order is issued and `fulfilled` when it is operational in higher-education institutions across the state.

## Why this matters in plain terms

The naming of the promise after a specific student carries a public commitment that the legislation and the redressal channel will be measured against the failures the case exposed. We will track that measurement as evidence appears.
