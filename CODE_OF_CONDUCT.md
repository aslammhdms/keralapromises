# Code of Conduct

Kerala Promises exists to record facts about public commitments, in a tone that the people of Kerala can trust. That work depends on contributors treating one another, and the public, with respect.

## What we expect

- **Stick to evidence.** Make claims through the source hierarchy in `METHODOLOGY.md`. If you cannot cite it, do not assert it.
- **Address the work, not the person.** Critique a status change, an evidence excerpt, a translation. Do not critique the contributor.
- **Use plain, neutral language.** No charged adjectives. No mockery. No sarcasm at the expense of a politician, a party, or another contributor. The voice rules in `BRIEF.md` Section 2 apply to issue comments and pull request reviews, not only to published copy.
- **Assume good faith.** Most disagreements on this project will be about the interpretation of evidence. Treat the other person as a fellow citizen who wants the record to be accurate.
- **Disclose conflicts.** If you work for a political party, a campaign, a government department, an evidence source, or an organisation whose work is mentioned in a promise you are editing, say so on the pull request.

## What we will not accept

- Personal attacks, harassment, or threats of any kind.
- Discrimination or slurs against any caste, religion, gender, language community, region, or political identity.
- Coordinated brigading, sock-puppeting, or use of automated accounts to influence the record.
- Publishing private information about a contributor or a member of the public without consent.
- Editing a promise's status in a way that is not supported by an admissible source, after being told so.

## How we enforce this

Editors moderate. A contributor who breaks the rules above receives, in order:

1. A correction on the relevant pull request or issue, with a link to the section of this document.
2. A short suspension from contributing.
3. A permanent ban.

Editors will use the lightest step that fits the situation, and will escalate only when behaviour is repeated or severe. Decisions are recorded publicly on the issue or pull request, so the record is auditable.

## How to report

If you witness or experience behaviour that breaks this code of conduct, email the editors at `conduct@keralapromises.in`. Reports are confidential. If your report concerns an editor, send it to one of the other editors listed on `/about`.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: this repository, issue and pull request discussions, the submission form, and any communication channel the editors use to coordinate work on the site.
