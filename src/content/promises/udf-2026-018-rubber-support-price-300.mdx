## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to an assured support price of ₹300 per kilogram for natural rubber under the state's rubber support price scheme. The promise is a specific price target.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether ₹300 is the price paid directly to farmers under the state's Rubber Support Price Scheme, or the headline support price net of any inter-departmental transfers.
- The procurement channel: Rubber Board co-operatives, the Kerala Rubber Limited proposal (tracked separately), or scheduled commercial banks under interest subvention.
- The phasing: a one-time hike to ₹300 from the existing support price, or staged increases across the term.
- The position on arrears in the existing rubber support price payments, which is widely discussed in coverage but not the subject of this promise.

We will record these as they appear in cabinet decisions and government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order raising the rubber support price toward ₹300 is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when ₹300 per kilogram is the operational support price at which natural rubber is procured under the official state scheme, with the order published in the gazette.

A partial hike short of ₹300 will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`. A revision to the formula that does not translate into the price actually paid to farmers will not count.

## Why this matters in plain terms

Rubber is the single largest plantation crop in Kerala and the price farmers receive is the central question for rubber-growing households across the central Kerala belt. The record matters.
