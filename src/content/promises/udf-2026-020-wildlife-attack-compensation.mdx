## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to a 50% increase in the ex-gratia compensation paid to families of persons who lose their lives in wildlife attacks. The promise is a specific increment to an existing rate.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The base rate against which the 50% increment is to be calculated.
- Whether the increase applies only to the central head of death compensation, or also to subsidiary heads (medical, injury, livestock, crop, property).
- The timeline for payments after a verified incident.
- Coordination with the existing tribunal arrangements proposed in the manifesto for human-wildlife conflict compensation.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order raising the ex-gratia rate is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when the new rate, at 50% over the prevailing rate at the time the manifesto was issued, is the operational rate and verified payments at the new rate have been made to bereaved families.

A revision below 50% will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.

## Why this matters in plain terms

The forest-fringe districts of Wayanad, Idukki, Palakkad, Kannur, and parts of Pathanamthitta and Kollam see fatal wildlife encounters every year. The ex-gratia rate is the state's measurable expression of how it values those losses. The record matters.
