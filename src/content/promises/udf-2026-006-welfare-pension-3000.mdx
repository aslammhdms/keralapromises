## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to raising welfare pensions to ₹3,000 per month, announced as one of the five "Indira guarantees" at the manifesto launch on 2 April 2026. The previous statutory rate, before the new government took office, was reported in manifesto-launch coverage as ₹2,000.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the new rate applies uniformly across the several welfare-pension categories (old-age, widow, disability, agricultural workers, unmarried-women, others) or differs by category.
- The phasing: a one-time hike to ₹3,000, or staged increases across the five-year term.
- The position on the parallel commitment to clear pension arrears, which is widely discussed in coverage but not assessed by this promise.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order raising the rate is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when ₹3,000 is the operational monthly rate paid to all eligible categories and beneficiaries report receipt at that rate, on the terms the operational order specifies.

A partial hike short of ₹3,000, or a hike applied to only some pension categories, will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.
