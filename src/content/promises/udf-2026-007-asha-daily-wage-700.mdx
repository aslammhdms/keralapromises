## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to a daily wage of ₹700 for ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist) workers across Kerala. ASHA workers are frontline public health workers, paid by the state government as honorarium rather than as salaried employees, and the daily-wage commitment is the manifesto's central labour assurance for the cadre.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the figure applies as a daily rate, a calculated monthly equivalent, or as task-based incentives at that floor.
- Whether arrears in the existing honorarium regime are to be cleared as part of the reform.
- Whether the new rate is statutory or contractual.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when any cabinet decision, government order, or budget head moves the honorarium toward the ₹700-per-day target. It reaches `fulfilled` when the operational rate, in daily-equivalent terms or as paid monthly, reaches the ₹700 floor across all serving ASHA workers in Kerala, with an order published in the gazette.

The 18 May 2026 cabinet hike of ₹3,000 per month moves ASHA pay forward but does not yet meet the ₹700-per-day target. A ₹12,000 monthly figure, taken at 25 working days, implies roughly ₹480 per day. The promise stays `in_progress` until the daily-equivalent rate or the monthly floor reaches the manifesto level.

## Why this matters in plain terms

ASHA workers staff Kerala's public health outreach: maternal and child health, immunisation drives, NCD screening, and household-level public-health communication. How they are paid is a labour-market signal as much as a public-health budget question. The record matters.
