## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to interest-free business loans of up to ₹5 lakh for young entrepreneurs, announced as one of the five "Indira guarantees" at the manifesto launch on 2 April 2026.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The eligible age band for "youth".
- Whether the scheme is universal or means-tested.
- Whether the loan is fully interest-free for the entire term or only for a defined subsidy window.
- Which lending channel disburses the loan: KFC, KSFE, scheduled commercial banks under government interest subvention, or a new corporation.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order constituting the scheme is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when the scheme is operational and eligible young entrepreneurs are receiving disbursements, on the terms the operational order specifies.

A pilot in a limited number of districts will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.
