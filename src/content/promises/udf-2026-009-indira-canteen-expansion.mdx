## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to expanding Indira Canteen services in Kerala as part of a stated objective of a hunger-free state. The Indira Canteen brand was first established in Karnataka in 2017 and is the canonical reference for subsidised cooked-meal outlets operated under direct government finance.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The target number of outlets and their distribution across panchayats, municipalities, and corporations.
- The subsidised price per meal and the categories of beneficiary (universal access, BPL-only, students, the urban homeless).
- Whether the canteens will be operated through Kudumbashree, a new public enterprise, or contracted operators.
- The relationship to the existing Kerala State Civil Supplies Corporation (Supplyco) and the Kudumbashree Janakeeya Hotel network.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order constituting the scheme is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when the network is operational at the scale the operational order specifies and meals are being served at the subsidised price.

A pilot in a limited number of districts will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.
