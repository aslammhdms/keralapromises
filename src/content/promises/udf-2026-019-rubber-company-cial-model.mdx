## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to setting up a Kerala Rubber Company on the lines of the Cochin International Airport Limited (CIAL) model. CIAL is the reference because it is the canonical example of a Kerala state-promoted public limited company with public shareholding, operating commercially under a government-anchored governance structure.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The capital structure: state share, public shareholding cap, role of farmer producer organisations, and any equity for the Rubber Board.
- The scope of the company's mandate: procurement only, or value-addition (latex concentrate, blocks, rubberised products), exports, R&D.
- The relationship to the existing Rubber Producers' Societies, the Rubber Board, and the proposed ₹300 support price scheme (tracked separately under udf-2026-018).
- The location of the headquarters and processing facilities.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order constituting the company is announced, or when an enabling Bill is introduced in the Assembly. It reaches `fulfilled` when the company is constituted under the Companies Act, has equity subscribed in line with the manifesto's CIAL-model commitment, and is operational with a public-shareholding offer or a board in place.

A study committee, a working group, or a renaming of an existing entity will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.

## Why this matters in plain terms

A state-promoted company on the CIAL model is structurally distinct from a co-operative federation or a directorate. The shareholding base, governance, and commercial mandate determine whether such a vehicle can stabilise rubber prices over a full price cycle. The record matters.
