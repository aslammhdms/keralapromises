## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to establishing a state Department of Peace and Harmony to address political violence in Kerala, with the manifesto citing a Rajasthan precedent for the institutional model.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the department will be stand-alone or housed within the Home department.
- Its operational mandate: investigation, mediation, monitoring, victim support, or a combination.
- The interaction with the Police, the existing Communal Harmony Cell, and any panchayat-level peace committees.
- Whether the model adapts the Rajasthan Department of Peace and Non-Violence or another precedent, and what specific features of that precedent are intended.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order constituting the department is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when a government order formally creates the department, allocates a budget head, names a head of department, and the order is published in the gazette.

A renaming or merging of an existing cell or directorate without a stand-alone departmental structure will not count as fulfilment.

## Why this matters in plain terms

Kerala has documented patterns of political violence in several districts over multiple decades. A department-level commitment shapes budget priority, staffing, and visibility of prevention work in policy. The record matters.
