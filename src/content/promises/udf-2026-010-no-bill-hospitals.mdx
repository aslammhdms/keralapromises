## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to establishing no-bill hospitals on the model of JIPMER, the Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education and Research in Pondicherry. The reference model is a tertiary public-sector teaching hospital that charges only nominal user fees at point of care, with the bulk of the cost absorbed by the centre.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The number of no-bill hospitals to be established and their geographic distribution.
- Whether the model will be applied to existing medical colleges, district hospitals, or as new institutions.
- The fiscal scaffolding: pooled insurance, state-budget subsidies, or central co-funding.
- The relationship to the parallel Oommen Chandy Health Insurance commitment tracked separately.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order constituting the scheme is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when at least one government hospital is operating on a verified no-bill basis under the scheme, with the operational details set out in a gazette notification.

A renaming of existing fee-charging hospitals will not count as fulfilment.
