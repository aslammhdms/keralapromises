## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to earmarking 5% of the state plan outlay for young people under an initiative branded Yuva Shakti Naadin Sampath. The promise is structural: an allocation rule, not a specific scheme.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The definition of "youth" used to compute the ring-fence.
- The basket of schemes counted toward the 5% threshold.
- Whether the floor applies from the first state budget under the new government or is phased.
- Reporting and audit arrangements that allow citizens to verify compliance year by year.

We will record these as they appear in budget documents and government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when the first state budget under the new government reports a specific Yuva Shakti Naadin Sampath head with the 5% commitment formally recognised. It reaches `fulfilled` when the actual allocation in a budget (not the budget estimate alone, but the revised estimate and actuals reported by the AG) meets or exceeds the 5% floor for a complete financial year.

A line-item rebadging of existing youth schemes without a net increase will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.
