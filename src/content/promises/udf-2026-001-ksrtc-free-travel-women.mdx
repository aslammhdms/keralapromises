## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to free bus travel for women on services operated by the Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC). The commitment is statewide in scope.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

Two operational details that will determine how the promise is fulfilled are not specified in the manifesto excerpt as we have it:

- Whether free travel applies only to ordinary services, or extends to long-distance, super-fast, and air-conditioned services as well.
- Whether eligibility is universal or means-tested in any way.

These will be clarified by government orders if and when the scheme is set up. We will record the operational rules as they appear in evidence, not before.

The first cabinet meeting on 18 May 2026 cleared the scheme for a 15 June 2026 start. The operational order has not yet been published, so the classes of service and any eligibility limits remain to be confirmed against a Tier 1 source.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when the government issues an order constituting the scheme. It reaches `fulfilled` when free travel is operational and women across the state can use it without paying, on the classes of service the operational order specifies.

A pilot in a limited number of districts will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.

## Why this matters in plain terms

KSRTC is the largest public transport operator in Kerala. Changing fare policy for half the state's adult population is a fiscal and operational decision that affects daily life, household budgets, and how women travel for work and study. The record matters.
