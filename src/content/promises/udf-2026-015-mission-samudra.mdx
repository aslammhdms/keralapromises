## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to a flagship initiative branded Mission Samudra, framed as an integrated programme to develop Kerala's 600-km coastline and 44 rivers, linking ports and inland water transportation as a maritime hub.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The constituent projects packaged under the Mission Samudra umbrella.
- The institutional anchor: a new mission, the Ports department, or a multi-department task force.
- The investment envelope and the split between state outlay, central support, and private capital.
- The relationship to the existing Vizhinjam and Cochin port ecosystem.
- The environmental safeguards and the position on the Coastal Regulation Zone notifications.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

This is a programme-level commitment, not a single scheme. Because it bundles several projects, fulfilment will be tracked as a set of sub-promises once the programme's operational order is issued. The umbrella reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order constituting Mission Samudra is announced. The umbrella reaches `fulfilled` only when its principal constituent projects, as specified in the operational order, are themselves fulfilled.
