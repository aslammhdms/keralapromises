## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to a paddy procurement price of ₹35 per kilogram. The promise is a specific price target rather than a structural reform.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the ₹35 figure refers to the full price paid to farmers, inclusive of any central MSP component plus state top-up, or only to the state's share.
- The procurement channel: Supplyco, paddy receipt sheets, panchayat-level cooperatives.
- The position on the existing arrears in paddy payments, which is widely discussed in agricultural coverage but not the subject of this promise.
- The phasing: a one-time hike to ₹35, or staged increases across the term.

We will record these as they appear in cabinet decisions and government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision or government order raising the procurement price toward ₹35 is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when ₹35 per kilogram is the operational rate at which paddy is procured across Kerala under the official procurement channel, with the order published in the gazette.

A partial hike short of ₹35 will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`. A change to the MSP top-up alone, without a matching increase in the price actually paid to farmers, will not count.
