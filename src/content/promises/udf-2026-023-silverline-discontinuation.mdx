## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to two paired actions on the SilverLine semi-high-speed rail project, which was advanced by the previous LDF government: the discontinuation of the project, and the denotification of the land already acquired or notified for acquisition along its alignment.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- The legal route: cabinet decision and government order, or a Bill repealing the K-Rail Special Purpose Vehicle's enabling instrument.
- The fate of survey stones, demolished or damaged structures, and partly disbursed compensation along the corridor.
- The position on any contractual commitments to consultants, contractors, or central authorities arising from the project's prior stages.
- Whether a successor mobility plan replaces the SilverLine corridor concept or the alignment is released entirely.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision discontinuing the project is announced, or when a government order initiating the denotification process is issued. It reaches `fulfilled` when both halves are met: the project is formally discontinued by a documented government order, and the land notifications are revoked by gazette notification with the corresponding entries restored in land records.

A pause, a review committee, or a freeze on further acquisition without denotification of land already notified will be recorded as `in_progress`, not `fulfilled`.

## Why this matters in plain terms

The SilverLine corridor passes through residential land, paddy fields, and dense urban frontage across eleven districts. The status of the land already notified for acquisition has direct consequences for thousands of households, regardless of which way the project goes. The record matters.
