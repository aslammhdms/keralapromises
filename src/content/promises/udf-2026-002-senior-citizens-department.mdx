## What was promised

The UDF 2026 manifesto commits to creating a dedicated department of the Government of Kerala for the welfare and protection of senior citizens. As written, the promise is structural: a new department, not a new scheme.

## What is unclear from the manifesto text alone

- Whether the department will be a stand-alone entity or sit under the existing Social Justice department.
- The scope of the department's remit: pensions only, or pensions plus care homes, helplines, elder-abuse protection, geriatric health coordination.
- The budget commitment and headcount.

We will record these as they appear in government orders.

The first cabinet meeting on 18 May 2026 approved formation of the department and committed to a two-month study of the Japanese elderly-care model. ANI wire copy carried by aggregator sites reports that a government order constituting the department was issued the day after the cabinet meeting, but neither the order number nor a gazette reference has been verified against a Tier 1 source on our list. The order, when verified, will determine whether the structural commitment (stand-alone department, budget head, head of department) has been met.

## How we will assess fulfilment

The promise reaches `in_progress` when a cabinet decision constituting the department is announced. It reaches `fulfilled` when a government order formally creates the department, allocates a budget head, and names a head of department, with the order published in the gazette.

A renaming of an existing department, or the creation of a "cell" or "directorate" within an existing department, will not count as fulfilment unless the structural commitment of a stand-alone department is also met.

## Why this matters in plain terms

Kerala has one of the oldest median populations in India. A department-level commitment shapes budget priority, staffing, and the visibility of senior citizens in policy. The record matters.
